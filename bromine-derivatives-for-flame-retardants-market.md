Polybrominated Diphenyl Ethers (PBDEs) are a group of compounds that were commonly used in electronic devices and textiles, but have been phased out due to environmental concerns. Hexabromocyclododecane (HBCDs) is another type of flame retardant used in building insulation materials. Tetrabromobisphenol A (TBBPA) is primarily employed in printed circuit boards and electrical products. Decabromodiphenyl Ethane (DBDPE) is a newer flame retardant that is increasingly used as a replacement for PBDEs. Other types of bromine derivatives can include alternative flame retardants and novel compounds that are being developed.</p></p>
