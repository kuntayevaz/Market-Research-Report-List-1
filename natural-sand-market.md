<p><p>Natural sand is a granular material composed of finely divided rock and mineral particles, primarily composed of silica. It is obtained from river beds, beaches, and sand dunes through natural erosion and sedimentation processes. Natural sand is widely used in various construction activities such as building, roads, bridges, and landscaping due to its desirable properties such as high compressive strength, durability, and good abrasion resistance. Additionally, its natural rounded grains allow for good workability and provide a pleasing aesthetic appearance.</p><p>The global natural sand market is anticipated to witness significant growth during the forecast period of 2021-2026. <p><p>The natural sand market includes three main types: river sand, mountain sand, and sea sand. River sand is sourced from riverbeds and has a smooth texture, making it suitable for construction purposes. Mountain sand is extracted from mountainous regions and is known for its coarse texture, which is often used in activities like landscaping. Sea sand, acquired from coastal areas, has fine granules and is primarily utilized in land reclamation projects or in the manufacturing of concrete. These different sand types cater to various industries depending on their physical characteristics and geological origin.</p></p>
