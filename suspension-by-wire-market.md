<p><p>Suspension-by-wire, also known as electronically-controlled suspension, is an advanced technology used in automobiles that replaces traditional mechanical spring and shock absorber suspension systems with electronically-controlled actuators. These actuators are connected to each wheel and are capable of adjusting the suspension settings in real-time based on driver inputs and road conditions. Suspension-by-wire offers improved ride comfort, safety, and handling by allowing for faster response times and precise control of suspension parameters.</p><p>The suspension-by-wire market is experiencing steady growth and is expected to continue expanding at a CAGR of 4.4% during the forecast period. <p><p>The Suspension-by-Wire market includes two notable types: the Active Roll Control (ARC) system and the Adaptive Damping System (ADS) market. The ARC system focuses on reducing body roll and improving vehicle stability during cornering by actively adjusting the suspension forces. On the other hand, the ADS market aims at enhancing the overall ride comfort and handling by dynamically adjusting the suspension damping in response to changing road conditions. These systems offer advanced features to enhance vehicle performance and provide a smoother and more controlled driving experience.</p></p>
