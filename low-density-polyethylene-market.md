<p><p>The Low Density Polyethylene (LDPE) market is comprised of two major types of manufacturing processes: Autoclave Process and Tubular Process. The Autoclave Process involves the polymerization of ethylene under high pressure and temperature in a stirred reactor, leading to the formation of LDPE. On the other hand, the Tubular Process involves the polymerization of ethylene in a series of tubular reactors, resulting in the production of LDPE. Both processes have their distinct advantages and are used in various industries for different applications of LDPE.</p></p>
