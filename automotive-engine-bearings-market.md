<p><p>Automotive engine bearings are vital components responsible for reducing friction and supporting the engine's moving parts. There are three main types of bearings used in the market. </p><p>1. Ball Bearings: Consist of steel balls held in a cage, enabling smooth rotary motion with minimal friction.</p><p>2. Plain Bearings: Utilize a sliding motion between two surfaces, often lubricated, to reduce friction and provide support.</p><p>3. Roller Bearings: Incorporate cylindrical rollers in a cage arrangement, delivering increased load capacity and better resistance to misalignment or impact loads compared to ball bearings.</p></p>
