<p><p>Hemoglobin A1c monitoring is a method used to track average blood sugar levels over 2-3 months in people with diabetes. The market for Hemoglobin A1c monitoring includes different types such as Ion-exchange, High-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC), Immunoassay, Boronate affinity chromatography, and Direct enzymatic assay. Ion-exchange and HPLC methods separate and measure different forms of hemoglobin. Immunoassay uses antibodies to detect and quantify hemoglobin A1c. Boronate affinity chromatography captures and measures hemoglobin A1c. Direct enzymatic assay uses specific enzymes to directly measure hemoglobin A1c levels in blood samples.</p></p>
