<p><p>Automatic Voltage Regulator (AVR) is a device that stabilizes electrical output by automatically regulating voltage fluctuations. The market is categorized into three types: linear regulator, shunt voltage regulator, and others. </p><p>Linear Regulator uses a linear semiconductor to maintain a stable output voltage, providing accurate regulation with minimal noise and high efficiency. </p><p>Shunt Voltage Regulator controls the voltage difference across a variable resistance, allowing it to maintain stability by diverting excess current away from the load.</p><p>The "Others" category includes various types of regulators like switching regulators, buck-boost regulators, and synchronous regulators that provide voltage regulation through different techniques.</p></p>
