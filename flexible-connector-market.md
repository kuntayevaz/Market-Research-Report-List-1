<p><p>A flexible connector, also known as an expansion joint, is a device used to join two parts of a system while allowing for movement, vibration, and thermal expansion. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 7.1% during the forecasted period. This growth can be attributed to several factors.</p><p>Firstly, the increasing demand from industries such as oil and gas, automotive, and aerospace is driving the market. Flexible connectors are used in these industries to transfer fluids, gases, and other substances safely and efficiently. The growth of these industries, especially in emerging economies, is expected to boost the demand for flexible connectors.</p><p>Secondly, the growing trend of miniaturization in electronics is also fueling the demand for flexible connectors. As electronic devices become smaller and more compact, the need for flexible connectors to accommodate movement and vibrations within these devices increases.</p><p>Additionally, the rising need for efficient energy transmission and distribution systems is driving the demand for flexible connectors in the power sector. These connectors are used in power plants, substations, and renewable energy installations to provide flexibility and reliability in the transmission of electricity.</p><p>Furthermore, stringent regulations regarding worker safety and environmental protection are also expected to drive the adoption of flexible connectors in various industries.</p><p>In conclusion, the flexible connector market is poised for significant growth in the coming years.
