<p><p>Superconductors are materials that conduct electricity without any resistance, resulting in high energy efficiency and low power loss. There are two types of superconductor markets: Low-Temperature Superconductor (LTS) and High-Temperature Superconductor (HTS). LTS are superconductors that operate at very low temperatures, generally below 30 Kelvin (-243 degrees Celsius). On the other hand, HTS are superconductors that can operate at relatively higher temperatures, above 30 Kelvin. The difference in temperature requirements between LTS and HTS determines their applications and potential uses in various industries.</p></p>
