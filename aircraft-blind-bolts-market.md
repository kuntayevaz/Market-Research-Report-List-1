<p><p>Aircraft blind bolts, also known as blind fasteners or blind rivets, are widely used in the aerospace industry for joining structural components without the need for access to both sides of the assembly. These bolts consist of a threaded body with a shank and a bulbous head, along with a mandrel or break stem that is inserted into the shank during installation. <p><ul><li>Drive-Nut Blind Bolts</li><li>Pull Type Blind Bolts</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Aircraft blind bolts are used in aviation industry for joining parts that are difficult to access from both sides. There are two main types of blind bolts: Drive-Nut blind bolts and Pull-Type blind bolts. Drive-Nut blind bolts have a threaded nut at one end that is driven into the bolt body, providing a secure connection. Pull-Type blind bolts have a collar on one end that is pulled into the bolt body, creating a locked joint. Both types are essential in aircraft assembly, ensuring strong and reliable connections even in hard-to-reach areas.</p></p>
