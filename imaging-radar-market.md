<p><p>Imaging radar is a technology used for detecting, locating, and tracking objects by using radar systems that produce images of the objects. There are different types of imaging radar systems available in the market. Millimeter-wave imaging radar uses high-frequency electromagnetic waves to capture detailed images, enabling applications like airport security and automotive collision avoidance. On the other hand, laser imaging radar (also known as lidar) employs laser pulses to create accurate 3D images, facilitating applications in autonomous vehicles, robotics, and environmental mapping. Both technologies have unique characteristics and applications within the field of imaging radar.</p></p>
