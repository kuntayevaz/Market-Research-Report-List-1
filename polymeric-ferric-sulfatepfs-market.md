<p><p>Polymeric Ferric Sulfate (PFS) is a type of coagulant used in water treatment processes. The market for PFS can be categorized into three types: Hydrogen Peroxide Oxidation, Potassium Chlorate (Sodium) Oxidation Method, and Sodium Hypochlorite Oxidation Method. </p><p>The Hydrogen Peroxide Oxidation method involves the reaction of PFS with hydrogen peroxide to form an effective coagulant. The Potassium Chlorate (Sodium) Oxidation Method utilizes the oxidation of PFS with potassium chlorate or sodium chlorate to produce a coagulant. Lastly, the Sodium Hypochlorite Oxidation Method involves the oxidation of PFS with sodium hypochlorite to obtain a coagulant. These methods provide options for water treatment facilities to choose the most suitable and effective PFS coagulant for their specific needs.</p></p>
