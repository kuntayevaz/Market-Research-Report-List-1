The growing geriatric population and increasing dental disorders drive the demand for these products.</p><p>Furthermore, the use of stabilized zirconia as an electrolyte material in solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs) holds immense potential. <p><p>The stabilized zirconia market consists of different types of zirconia materials, including YSZ (yttria-stabilized zirconia), CSZ (ceria-stabilized zirconia), MSZ (magnesia-stabilized zirconia), and ASZ (alumina-stabilized zirconia). YSZ contains yttria, which enhances its mechanical and thermal stability, making it suitable for applications like solid oxide fuel cells and oxygen sensors. CSZ, with cerium oxide, offers high oxygen conductivity, making it ideal for oxygen separation membranes. MSZ, with magnesium oxide, exhibits excellent thermal insulation properties, making it suitable for protective coatings and high-temperature applications. ASZ, with alumina, offers superior wear resistance and is used in applications such as cutting tools and valves.</p></p>
