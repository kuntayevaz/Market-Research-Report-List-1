<p><p>Molecular sieves are synthetic crystalline substances with highly uniform pores that can selectively adsorb certain molecules while excluding others. The market for molecular sieves can be broadly classified into three types: 3A, 4A, and 13X. </p><p>3A molecular sieves have a pore size that can selectively adsorb molecules up to a diameter of 3 Angstroms. They are commonly used for the dehydration of gases and liquids, as well as for the removal of certain impurities.</p><p>4A molecular sieves have a slightly larger pore size, allowing them to adsorb molecules up to 4 Angstroms in diameter. They are widely utilized for the drying and purification of gases and liquids, such as natural gas and petrochemicals.</p><p>13X molecular sieves have an even larger pore size, enabling them to adsorb molecules up to 10 Angstroms in diameter. These sieves are primarily used in applications involving the separation and removal of carbon dioxide, water, and sulfur compounds from various gas and liquid streams.</p><p>In addition to these types, there are other molecular sieves available in the market with varying pore sizes and specific applications. These other types of molecular sieves cater to specific industry needs, such as the removal of mercury or the purification of air for medical applications.</p></p>
