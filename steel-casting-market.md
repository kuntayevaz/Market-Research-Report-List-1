Carbon steel casting is made up of iron and carbon, suitable for applications requiring strength and hardness. Low-alloy steel casting contains additional alloying elements like manganese, nickel, or chromium, providing enhanced properties such as increased strength and corrosion resistance. High-alloy steel casting incorporates a significant amount of alloying elements, such as titanium or molybdenum, offering exceptional strength, heat resistance, and wear resistance. These different types cater to diverse industrial requirements.</p></p>
