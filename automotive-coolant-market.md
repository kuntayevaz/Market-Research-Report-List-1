<p><p>Automotive coolant, also known as antifreeze, is a fluid that is used to regulate the engine temperature in vehicles. It is typically a mixture of ethylene or propylene glycol and water, with additives to prevent corrosion, cavitation, and freezing. The primary function of automotive coolant is to absorb and transfer heat away from the engine, ensuring optimal performance and preventing overheating.</p><p>The automotive coolant market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the rising demand for vehicles worldwide. As the automotive industry continues to expand, the need for efficient and reliable engine cooling solutions increases.
