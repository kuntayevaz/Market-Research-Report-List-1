<p><p>Closed-cell extruded polystyrene foam (XPS) is a type of rigid insulation board made from polystyrene polymers. It is manufactured by extruding polystyrene through a die with different blowing agents, resulting in a closed-cell structure that provides excellent thermal insulation properties and moisture resistance. XPS foam is commonly used in the construction industry for insulation applications in roofs, walls, floors, and foundations.</p><p>The current outlook for the closed-cell extruded polystyrene foam market is positive, driven by the increasing demand for energy-efficient insulation materials in the construction sector. XPS foam offers superior thermal performance, high compressive strength, and durability, making it an ideal choice for insulation in both residential and commercial buildings. The growing focus on reducing energy consumption and improving building efficiency is expected to further boost the demand for XPS foam in the coming years.</p><p>Additionally, the rising adoption of green building materials and stringent government regulations regarding energy efficiency and sustainability are driving the market growth. XPS foam meets the requirements of several green building certification programs, which further enhances its market potential.</p><p>Furthermore, the construction industry's rapid growth, particularly in developing economies, coupled with increasing investments in infrastructure projects, will contribute to the market's expansion. The versatility of XPS foam, its ease of installation, and its ability to withstand high loads make it suitable for a wide range of applications, thereby propelling its demand.</p><p>Overall, the closed-cell extruded polystyrene foam market is expected to experience steady growth in the forecasted period, with a projected Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 14%.
