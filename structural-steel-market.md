<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Structural steel is categorized into various market types based on their shape and design. I-Beams are horizontal beams with a tapered top and bottom flange, providing great structural support. Angle (L-shape) steel is formed by bending a flat steel piece at a right angle, commonly used for framework and bracing. Hollow Structural Section (HSS) Shape refers to hollow steel tubes with various shapes like circular, square, or rectangular, used for columns and beams. Z-Shapes and T-Shapes are formed by bending flat steel pieces into a Z or T shape, suitable for load-bearing applications.</p></p>
