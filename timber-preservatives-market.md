<p><p>Timber preservatives are substances used to protect wood from damage caused by insects, decay, and weathering. There are three main types of timber preservatives: water-based, oil-based, and solvent-based. Water-based preservatives are environmentally friendly and are easy to apply, penetrating deep into the wood. Oil-based preservatives provide effective protection against decay and insect attack, while also improving the wood's appearance. Solvent-based preservatives are commonly used for outdoor applications as they offer a high level of protection and are resistant to moisture. Each type of preservative has its own advantages and is suitable for different applications in the timber industry.</p></p>
