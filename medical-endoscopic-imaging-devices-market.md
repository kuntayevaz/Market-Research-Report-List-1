<p><p>Medical endoscopic imaging devices are used in minimally invasive procedures to visualize internal body structures. Rigid endoscopes are straight and have a fixed shape, allowing direct visualization of specific areas. They are commonly used in procedures like arthroscopy or laparoscopy. On the other hand, flexible endoscopes have a flexible shaft, enabling more maneuverability and access to difficult-to-reach areas. They are often used in procedures like bronchoscopy or colonoscopy. Both types play a crucial role in diagnostic and therapeutic interventions by providing real-time imaging to guide medical professionals.</p></p>
