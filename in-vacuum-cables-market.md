<p><p>In the market for in-vacuum cables, there are two main types: high in-vacuum cables and UHV in-vacuum cables. High in-vacuum cables are designed to operate in environments with moderate levels of vacuum, typically up to 10^-6 Torr. They are commonly used in scientific research and industrial applications. On the other hand, UHV (Ultra-High Vacuum) in-vacuum cables are built to withstand extremely low levels of vacuum, usually up to 10^-11 Torr. These cables are predominantly used in highly specialized fields like semiconductors, accelerators, and space exploration.</p></p>
