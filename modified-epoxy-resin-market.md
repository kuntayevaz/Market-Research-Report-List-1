<p><p>The modified epoxy resin market consists of two major types: glycidyl ether resins and glycidyl lipid market. Glycidyl ether resins are formed by reacting phenols, alcohols, or amines with epichlorohydrin. They possess high mechanical strength, thermal stability, and chemical resistance. On the other hand, glycidyl lipid market involves the modification of epoxy resins using lipids, such as vegetable oils or animal fats. This modification enhances the flexibility and impact resistance of the epoxy resins, making them suitable for various applications in coatings, adhesives, and composites industries.</p></p>
