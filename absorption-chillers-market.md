Unlike traditional vapor compression chillers that rely on compressors, absorption chillers use an absorption cycle where a heat source is required to drive the refrigeration process. These chillers are particularly beneficial in scenarios where waste heat or low-grade heat is readily available, such as industrial processes or geothermal energy, making them energy-efficient and environmentally friendly alternatives.</p><p>The absorption chillers market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to rising environmental concerns and the need for energy conservation. <p><ul><li>Single Stage</li><li>Double Stage</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Absorption chillers are used for cooling purposes and are classified into two main market types: Single Stage and Double Stage. Single Stage absorption chillers use a single heat source and a refrigerant that evaporates at low temperature, while Double Stage absorption chillers use two heat sources and two refrigerants with different boiling points. Single Stage absorption chillers are simpler and less expensive, but Double Stage absorption chillers are more efficient and provide greater temperature differentials, making them suitable for larger-scale applications.</p></p>
