<p><p>Car DVRs, also known as dashboard cameras or black boxes, are devices used to record footage while driving. There are two main market types for Car DVRs: Single Channel and Dual Channel. Single Channel Car DVRs capture video in a forward-facing direction only, providing coverage of the road ahead. On the other hand, Dual Channel Car DVRs consist of two cameras, typically with one camera capturing the front view and the other camera recording the inside of the vehicle. This dual setup ensures both the view of the road and the cabin are recorded simultaneously.</p></p>
