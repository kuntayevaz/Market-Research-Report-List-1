<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Sperm separation systems are categorized into three types: on-chip, electrophoresis, and dielectrophoresis. On-chip systems use microfluidic devices to separate sperm based on their size, shape, or velocity. Electrophoresis relies on the principle of electrical charges to separate sperm based on their motility and charge. Dielectrophoresis separates sperm using an electric field gradient that interacts with the cell's polarizability. These systems play a significant role in assisted reproductive technologies by selecting high-quality sperm for various applications, such as in vitro fertilization.</p></p>
