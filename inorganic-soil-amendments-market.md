<p><ul><li>Dry</li><li>Liquid</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Inorganic soil amendments are classified into two types: dry and liquid. Dry soil amendments are typically in powdered or granular form and need to be mixed into the soil before planting. They are convenient to use and can be easily stored. On the other hand, liquid soil amendments are in liquid form and can be directly applied to the soil or mixed with water for irrigation. They offer quick and effective results as they are quickly absorbed by the plants. The choice between dry and liquid amendments depends on individual preferences and the specific needs of the soil and plants.</p></p>
