<p><p>Light Sheet Fluorescence Microscopy (LSFM) is a imaging technique used in biological research that enables three-dimensional imaging of samples. <p><p>Light Sheet Fluorescence Microscopy (LSFM) is an advanced imaging technique that allows scientists to visualize biological samples with minimal phototoxicity and high-speed imaging capabilities. LSFM includes various types, such as Single Plane Illumination Microscopy (SPIM), Dual-View Inverted Selective Plane Illumination Microscopy (DiSPIM), and others. SPIM involves illuminating a single plane within the sample, capturing images from different angles to reconstruct a 3D image. DiSPIM uses dual-view imaging to reduce scattering and increase resolution. Other LSFM variants utilize different illumination techniques and imaging configurations to suit specific research needs.</p></p>
