Direct printing involves directly applying color to the fabric using techniques like inkjet printing or screen printing. This allows for precise and intricate designs. Resist printing, on the other hand, involves creating a barrier on the fabric to prevent color from reaching certain areas. This can be done through techniques like wax resist or block printing. It allows for creating patterns with contrasting colors. Both direct printing and resist printing are widely used in the textile industry for various applications.</p></p>
