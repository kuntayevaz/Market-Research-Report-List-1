<p><p>The biohazard bags market consists of different types of materials like LDPE, HDPE, cellophane, and polypropylene. LDPE bags are made from low-density polyethylene and are commonly used for general purposes due to their flexibility and low cost. HDPE bags are made from high-density polyethylene and are preferred for their durability and resistance to tearing. Cellophane bags are made from a transparent, easily biodegradable material and are mainly used in food packaging. Polypropylene bags are made from a thermoplastic polymer and offer excellent strength and resistance to biological substances, making them suitable for biohazardous waste disposal.</p></p>
