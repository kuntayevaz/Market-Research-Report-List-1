<p><p>Digital PCR (dPCR) is a technique used in molecular biology to accurately quantify and analyze DNA or RNA molecules through partitioning individual samples into thousands or millions of separate reactions. This method increases the sensitivity and precision of DNA analysis. There are two main types of dPCR: Microfluidic dPCR and Droplet Digital PCR (ddPCR). Microfluidic dPCR utilizes tiny chambers on a microchip to partition DNA molecules, while ddPCR uses emulsion droplets. Both techniques have contributed to the growth of the dPCR market, providing researchers with more reliable and efficient methods for genetic analysis.</p></p>
