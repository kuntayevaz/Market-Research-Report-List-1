<p><p>The marine gas turbine market encompasses three distinct types of turbines: aeroderivative gas turbines, light-duty gas turbines, and heavy-duty gas turbines. Aeroderivative gas turbines are compact and versatile, often derived from aircraft engines, and are used to power smaller marine vessels. Light-duty gas turbines are typically used in mid-sized ships and offer a balance between compactness and power output. Heavy-duty gas turbines, on the other hand, are larger and more powerful, used in large vessels such as aircraft carriers and cruise ships to provide high levels of energy for propulsion and on-board systems.</p></p>
